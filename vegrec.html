Ragi Dosa

Ingredients:
  • 1 cup ragi flour (finger millet flour)
  • 1/2 cup rice flour
  • 1/4 cup curd (yogurt)
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed
  • Oil for cooking
Instructions:
  1. In a mixing bowl, combine ragi flour, rice flour, and salt.
  2. Add curd and mix well. Gradually add water to make a smooth batter of pouring consistency.
  3. Stir in chopped onion, green chilies, cumin seeds, and a pinch of asafoetida.
  4. Heat a non-stick pan and lightly grease it with oil.
  5. Pour a ladleful of batter and spread it gently into a thin circle.
  6. Cook on medium heat until the edges start lifting and the dosa turns crisp.
  7. Flip if necessary and cook the other side for a minute.
  8. Serve hot with coconut chutney or sambar.

Vegetable Stir Fry

Ingredients:
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (bell peppers, broccoli, carrots, tomato, onion)
  • 1 cup paneer, cubed
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Sesame seeds for garnish (optional)
Instructions:
  1. Heat olive oil in a pan over medium heat.
  2. Add minced garlic and sauté for 1 minute.
  3. Add cubed paneer and cook for 2-3 minutes until slightly golden.
  4. Add mixed vegetables and stir-fry for 5-7 minutes until tender.
  5. Pour in soy sauce, and season with salt and pepper.
  6. Cook for another 2 minutes, then serve hot, garnished with sesame seeds.

Chickpea Salad

Ingredients:
  • 1 can chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 tomato, diced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 1/4 red onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish (optional)
  • Fresh coriander (cilantro) for garnish (optional)
Instructions:
  1. In a large bowl, combine chickpeas, tomato, bell pepper, and red onion.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, lemon juice, salt, and pepper.
  3. Pour the dressing over the salad and toss to combine.
  4. Garnish with fresh parsley and coriander if desired, and serve chilled.

Vegetable Pulao

Ingredients:
  • 1 cup basmati rice
  • 1 tablespoon ghee or oil
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup carrots and beans, chopped
  • 1/2 cup peas
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 2 cups water
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander for garnish
Instructions:
  1. Wash the rice thoroughly and soak it in water for 15-20 minutes, then drain.
  2. Heat ghee or oil in a large pan. Add cumin seeds, cinnamon stick, cloves, and bay leaf. Sauté for a few seconds.
  3. Add sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.
  4. Add chopped carrots, beans, and peas. Sauté for 2-3 minutes.
  5. Stir in turmeric powder, garam masala, and green chilies.
  6. Add the soaked rice and stir gently to combine with the vegetables and spices.
  7. Pour in water and add salt. Bring to a boil, then cover and reduce the heat to low.
  8. Cook for 15-20 minutes, or until the rice is cooked and water is absorbed.
  9. Garnish with fresh coriander and serve hot.

Vegetable Curry

Ingredients:
  • 1 tablespoon coconut oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, grated
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, potatoes)
  • 1 cup paneer, cubed
  • 1 can coconut milk
  • 2 tablespoons curry powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish (optional)
Instructions:
  1. Heat coconut oil in a pot over medium heat.
  2. Add onion, garlic, and ginger; sauté until onion is translucent.
  3. Add mixed vegetables and curry powder; stir to coat.
  4. Pour in coconut milk and bring to a simmer.
  5. Add the cubed paneer and cook for an additional 10-15 minutes until vegetables are tender and paneer is heated through; season with salt.
  6. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve with rice or naan.

Roasted Vegetable Salad

Ingredients:
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (e.g., bell peppers, zucchini, carrots)
  • 2 cups leafy vegetables (e.g., spinach, kale, or Swiss chard)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • Salt to taste
  • Pepper to taste
Instructions:
  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss mixed vegetables with olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  3. Spread the mixed vegetables on a baking sheet and roast in the oven for 20-25 minutes or until tender.
  4. In the last 5 minutes of roasting, add the leafy vegetables to the baking sheet to wilt them slightly.
  5. Remove from the oven, mix with lemon juice, and serve.

Vegetable Khichdi

Ingredients:
  • 1 cup rice
  • 1/2 cup moong dal
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (e.g., peas, beans, bell peppers)
  • 1 carrot, chopped
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, chopped
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tablespoon ghee or olive oil
  • Fresh coriander, chopped (for garnish)
Instructions:
  1. Wash rice and moong dal together and soak for 30 minutes.
  2. In a pressure cooker, heat ghee or olive oil and add cumin seeds.
  3. Add chopped ginger, chopped carrot, and mixed vegetables; sauté for a few minutes.
  4. Add soaked rice and dal, turmeric, salt, and enough water.
  5. Cook for 2-3 whistles and let the pressure release naturally before serving.
  6. Garnish with fresh coriander before serving.

Sama Rice

Ingredients:
  • 1 cup sama rice
  • 2 cups water
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 1/4 cup peas (fresh or frozen)
  • 2 tablespoons cashews
  • 1-2 green chilies, slit (adjust to taste)
  • 8-10 curry leaves
  • 1 tablespoon ghee or oil (optional, for sautéing)
Instructions:
  1. Rinse the sama rice thoroughly and set aside.
  2. In a pot, heat ghee or oil (if using) and add the chopped onion, green chilies, and curry leaves. Sauté until the onion is translucent.
  3. Add the chopped tomato and peas; cook until the tomato softens.
  4. Add the rinsed sama rice, water, and salt. Bring to a boil.
  5. Add the cashews, reduce heat to low, cover, and cook until the rice is tender (about 15-20 minutes).
  6. Fluff with a fork and serve hot.

Millet Dosa

Ingredients:
  • Millet flour
  • Rice flour
  • Urad dal flour
  • Salt
  • Water
Instructions:
  1. Mix millet flour, rice flour, urad dal flour, and salt in a bowl.
  2. Add water gradually to make a smooth batter.
  3. Heat a non-stick pan, pour a ladle of batter, and spread it into a circle.
  4. Cook until golden brown on both sides and serve with chutney.

Jowar Roti

Ingredients:
  • Jowar flour
  • Water
  • Salt
Instructions:
  1. In a bowl, mix jowar flour and salt.
  2. Gradually add water to form a soft dough.
  3. Divide the dough into small balls and roll them out into flat discs.
  4. Cook on a hot tava until both sides are cooked and serve hot.

Oats Upma

Ingredients:
  • 1 cup oats
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (e.g., carrots, bell peppers, beans)
  • 1/2 cup peas (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1-2 green chilies, chopped (adjust to taste)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1-2 tablespoons oil
  • Water as needed
Instructions:
  1. Heat oil in a pan, add chopped onions and green chilies, and sauté until the onions are translucent.
  2. Add mixed vegetables and peas; cook for a few minutes until they are tender.
  3. Stir in oats and salt, and add water as needed to achieve your desired consistency.
  4. Cook until the oats are soft and the mixture is well combined. Serve hot.

Oats Idli

Ingredients:
  • 1 cup oats
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 1/2 cup water (adjust as needed)
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (e.g., carrots, peas, bell peppers, finely chopped)
  • 1/2 cup leafy vegetables (e.g., spinach or fenugreek leaves, chopped)
Instructions:
  1. Grind oats into a fine powder.
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine oats powder, yogurt, water, and salt to form a smooth batter.
  3. Add the mixed vegetables and leafy vegetables to the batter. Mix well.
  4. Add baking soda and mix until well combined.
  5. Pour the batter into idli molds and steam for about 10-15 minutes or until cooked through.
  6. Allow to cool slightly before removing from molds. Serve hot with chutney or sambar.

Sprouts Salad

Ingredients:
  • 1 cup mixed sprouts
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 1 tomato, diced
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 carrot, grated
  • 1/2 cup pomegranate seeds
  • 2 tablespoons fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • 1/4 cup groundnuts (peanuts), roasted and roughly chopped
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • Salt to taste
  • Pepper to taste
Instructions:
  1. In a bowl, combine mixed sprouts, cucumber, tomato, onion, grated carrot, pomegranate seeds, and chopped coriander.
  2. Add the roasted groundnuts for crunch.
  3. Drizzle with lemon juice, and season with salt and pepper.
  4. Toss well to combine all ingredients and serve fresh.

Barley Khichdi

Ingredients:
  • 1 cup barley
  • 1/2 cup moong dal
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons ghee
  • Spices (e.g., cumin, turmeric, garam masala, to taste)
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons fresh coriander leaves, chopped
Instructions:
  1. Rinse barley and moong dal thoroughly.
  2. In a pressure cooker, heat ghee and sauté onions with spices until golden brown.
  3. Add barley, moong dal, and water (about 4 cups). Stir well.
  4. Cook for a few whistles until the barley and dal are tender.
  5. Once done, garnish with fresh coriander and serve hot.

Chia Pudding

Ingredients:
  • Chia seeds
  • Almond milk
  • Sweetener
  • Vanilla extract
  • Fruits for topping
Instructions:
  1. In a bowl, combine chia seeds, almond milk, sweetener, and vanilla extract.
  2. Mix well and let it sit for about 5 minutes.
  3. Stir again to prevent clumping.
  4. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight to thicken.
  5. Once set, serve chilled, topped with your choice of fruits.
